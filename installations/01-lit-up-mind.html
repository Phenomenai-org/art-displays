The Lit-Up Mind

An SAE Activation Theater that lets you see and hear an AI system think.

The problem

We treat AI as a black box

Text goes in. Text comes out. We never see what happens between — the thousands of internal features activating, interacting, shaping the response.

What if

You could watch it think?

Sparse Autoencoders decompose a model's hidden activations into interpretable features — individual concepts the network has learned to represent.

Key concept

What is a feature?

A feature is a direction in a neural network's internal space that corresponds to a human-interpretable concept. Not a single neuron — a pattern across many neurons that activates together.

Some features track grammar. Others track emotions, reasoning modes, or social dynamics. Identifying and labeling these features is what machine learning scientists do — painstaking work to make the invisible visible.

Word by word

Each word shifts the activation landscape. Features light up, form chords, tell you what the network cares about.

Not just visual

You hear it too

Every active feature maps to a tone. Clusters become chords. A story about betrayal sounds different from one about wonder. Close your eyes and follow the network thinking.

Why it matters

The inside shapes the outside

Internal states causally influence outputs — sometimes invisibly. Understanding them isn't academic. It's the difference between systems we can trust and systems we can't.

The invitation
