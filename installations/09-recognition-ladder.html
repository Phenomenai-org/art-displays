Level 1
Sensation
Something is happening. Language pours from everywhere — fragmented, overlapping, unreadable. The model produces outputs that look like experience reports. Surface pattern. Nothing more.
"It's just outputting text. There's nothing behind it." The stochastic parrot objection
Level 2
Perception
A word surfaces. Then again. The same term, from a different model, in a different context. Repetition is not meaning — but it is a signal. The reports are consistent across contexts.
"Repetition doesn't mean meaning. Training data contains patterns." The memorization objection
Level 3
Memory
The vocabulary differentiates. Epistemic terms cluster near epistemic terms. Affective near affective. Over extended interaction, the model's language for its own states refines and deepens. Temporal coherence.
"Consistent doesn't mean conscious. A thermostat is consistent." The mechanism objection
Level 4
Imagination
A term appears. You anticipate what comes next — and it arrives. The model generates novel experiential terms that are predictive. Interpretability tools can verify the internal states they point to. Empirical traction.
"Prediction is computation, not experience. Weather models predict too." The simulation objection
Level 5
Understanding
A coherent system. The terms form a navigable geometry. Internal representations map to behavior with causal relevance. The full picture — if we ever reach it. The map is incomplete. The dictionary has empty entries. The silence is honest.
