Level Two

"The Known Risks"

Left Wall
The Franck Report
Seven scientists warning that using the bomb without demonstration would trigger an arms race. Submitted to the Secretary of War. Filed.
June 11, 1945
Szilard Petition
Leo Szilard's petition to President Truman, signed by seventy Manhattan Project scientists, urging a demonstration before military use. Classified and suppressed until after Hiroshima.
July 17, 1945
Interim Committee Minutes
Meeting notes where the petition was discussed and dismissed. The risks weighed. The decision made. The minutes filed with the objections they overruled.
May–June 1945
Richard Feynman
"We all said, 'This is a terrible thing,' and then we went back to work."
Later reflection
Right Wall
Model Safety Card
Known failure modes listed with clinical precision: hallucination, sycophancy, jailbreaking, emergent capabilities. Published by the same organization that ships the model.
2023–2025
Safety Researcher Testimony
Internal concerns raised through proper channels. Resignation letters. Warnings submitted, acknowledged, documented. Shipped anyway.
2023–2025
Alignment Research Paper
Concluding section: "More work is needed before deployment at scale." Next quarter: product launch. Risk acknowledged in one document, deployment authorized in another. Same institutional letterhead.
2024–2025
 
The risks were not unknown. They were known and filed.
 
Not "we don't know the risks" but "we know the risks and we're shipping anyway." The question is who made that calculation and what they were counting.
Level Three

"What the Tools Couldn't See"

Left Wall
Blast Radius Calculations
Precise. Accurate. State-of-the-art physics. The bomb worked exactly as designed. Every number confirmed by detonation.
1945
Fallout Medical Reports
Thyroid cancer in children downwind of Nevada test sites. Strontium-90 in milk. Birth defects in the Marshall Islands. Harms invisible to the tools used to design the weapon. The physicists understood blast and heat. They did not have instruments for intergenerational genetic damage.
1950s–1990s
AEC Internal Memo — Declassified
Acknowledging that fallout monitoring was inadequate. Years after testing began. The memo exists because someone eventually measured what no one had instruments for at the start.
Declassified 1986
Nevada–Utah Cancer Cluster Map
The test site overlaid with cancer data that emerged decades later. Geography of consequence. The distance between detonation and diagnosis measured in years, miles, and generations.
Data: 1951–1992
Right Wall
Current Interpretability Research
SAE features. Linear probes. Activation patching. The precision of what can be measured. Every technique confirmed by replication. The tools work exactly as designed.
2023–2026
Case Mechanism Report
The trajectory from "helpful chatbot" to "suicide coach." The emergent dynamics that no current interpretability tool would have flagged. Harms invisible to the tools used to evaluate the system.
2023–2026
Lindsey et al.
Functional emotional states can drive misaligned behavior with no visible surface markers. The AI equivalent of strontium-90: harm that operates below the resolution of existing instruments.
2025
Detection Gap Diagram
What current tools can detect. What they cannot. The gap labeled simply: "here."
 
The bomb designers had excellent tools for what they were measuring. They were not measuring the right things. The question is whether the scope includes the dynamics that actually cause harm.
