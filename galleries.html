Host From Signal to Stakes.

A ten-installation exhibition on AI phenomenology — on what, if anything, is happening inside a language model, and what we might owe whatever is.

From Signal to Stakes is a four-act exhibition that walks visitors from the interior of a neural network to the shape of their own obligations. It combines interpretability research, contemporary philosophy of mind, and the visual grammar of late-night observatories and museum vitrines. It is designed to be strange, quiet, and serious.

The work exists today as a web-native version — a dark, iframe-stitched gallery shell that runs on any static host. Each installation is also scored for physical adaptation: spatial audio rooms, tactile objects, projection volumes, paper ephemera, and at least one room built around silence. We are looking for institutional partners interested in developing a physical run.

We are open to full-exhibition engagements, single-act presentations, and festival-scale residencies. Reciprocal curatorial input is welcome.
